Polyaspartic vs. Epoxy Garage Floors

Polyaspartic (a type of polyurea) is the modern alternative to epoxy. It's prized for a fast cure, UV stability, and flexibility that resists the peeling and chipping epoxy can develop over time.

Where Polyaspartic Wins

  • Cures fast — many garages are done and back in use in a single day
  • UV-stable — it won't yellow in sunlight like epoxy can
  • Flexible and abrasion-resistant — less prone to chipping and peeling
  • Works in a wider temperature range, including cooler installs

The Trade-off

Polyaspartic costs more than epoxy and cures so quickly that it takes an experienced installer to apply it well. That's exactly why matching with a vetted local pro matters.

The System Most Pros Recommend

For most residential garages, the strongest setup is an epoxy base coat with a polyaspartic topcoat — you get epoxy's bond and polyaspartic's speed and durability. Compare quotes to see what local installers recommend for your floor.
