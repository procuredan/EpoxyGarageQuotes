Cost Guide

How Much Does a Garage Floor Coating Cost?

A professional garage floor coating typically costs between $3 and $7 per square foot installed. For a standard 2-car garage (about 400–500 sq. ft.), that usually works out to roughly $1,600–$3,300, depending on the coating system, the condition of your concrete, and the finish you choose.

What Drives the Price

  • Garage size — 1-car, 2-car, 3-car, or a larger commercial floor
  • Coating system — epoxy, polyaspartic/polyurea, or a hybrid (epoxy base + polyaspartic topcoat)
  • Concrete prep — diamond grinding, crack and pit repair, and moisture testing
  • Finish — solid color, decorative flake, or a premium metallic
  • Condition — heavily stained, cracked, or previously coated floors take more prep

Why Professional Coatings Cost More Than a DIY Kit

Big-box epoxy kits look cheap up front, but they are thin, single-coat products applied over lightly prepped concrete — which is why they commonly peel within a couple of years. A professional installer grinds the slab, repairs it, and applies a thicker, stronger system that is built to last 15+ years. The prep is the part you're really paying for.
